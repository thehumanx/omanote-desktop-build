# The daily canvas

The canvas is omanote's home. It shows **one day at a time** — every todo, note, bookmark, and event tied to that day, together in one scrollable feed. It's where you capture things, and where the day comes back together when you look at it later.

## One day, everything together

Most apps split your day across separate lists. The canvas doesn't: a quick note, the link you saved, the task you finished, and the thing that happened all sit side by side, in the order they belong to you. That mix *is* the record of your day.

## Moving between days

The date strip at the top moves you backward and forward through your days. Today is where you capture; past days are a faithful record you can revisit anytime; future days are where scheduled todos and reminders wait for you.

## Arranging your day

Items on the canvas can be reordered by dragging, so the most important things sit where you want them. Your arrangement is saved per day. If you prefer a cleaner backdrop, you can toggle the dot grid on or off in Settings → Appearance.

## What lands here

Anything you capture from the composer shows up on the canvas for the current day. Some things also appear on other days — a todo shows on its due date, and a recurring todo appears on every day it's due. Completing a todo logs it into your timeline, which also surfaces here as an event.
